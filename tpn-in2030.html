Two engagements for cross-Atlantic policy organisations: real-time AI summaries of summit talks, debates, and committee sessions — turning hours of live proceedings into structured, searchable briefings. Both apps share a common architecture; the contexts they serve are distinct.

01 / Engagement
Transatlantic Policy
Network
tpnonline.org — EU/US politicians, business leaders & think tanks
Demo scaffolding
Policy Live AI EU / US Summaries

The Transatlantic Policy Network links elected officials, business associations, and think tanks across the EU and US. Their summits and working-group sessions generate dense, fast-moving oral content that attendees and staff struggle to capture in real time.

The app ingests live transcription from summit talks and debates, chunks it into topic segments, and produces structured summaries with speaker attribution, key positions, and points of agreement or tension — all surfaced in near-real-time during the event.

Context — TPN runs the Transatlantic Vision 2030 programme, a multi-year initiative aligning EU and US policy priorities. This tool was built to reduce the manual burden on TPN staff during high-volume conference days.

02 / Engagement
Ideas Network 2030
Steering committee minutes & event summaries
Demo scaffolding
Governance Live AI Committee Minutes

Ideas Network 2030 brings together senior figures to shape long-range policy thinking on transatlantic priorities. Steering committee sessions are intensive: high participant counts, overlapping threads, and consequential decisions that need to be accurately recorded.

The app applies the same live-summary architecture as the TPN tool, adapted for committee-room dynamics — action-item extraction, decision logging, and a running summary of open questions alongside the narrative digest of each session.

Context — IN2030 is likely connected to TPN’s Transatlantic Vision 2030 programme. The two tools share a backend; this instance is configured for closed committee sessions rather than open-stage summits.
